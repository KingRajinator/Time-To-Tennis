Meet Our Coaches

HELP IS ALWAYS APPRECIATED!

Shriya Challam is a coach for the Seattle branch who teaches young kids to learn the basics of tennis and helps teach during events. Shriya loves to work with kids and help the community with her skills. She is excited to work with a variety of students to share her passion for tennis with others.

Sahithi Challam is a coach for the Seattle branch who inspires to expand the tennis community with her tennis skills and her love for tennis. She works with many students around the area to help improve others' tennis and enjoys encouraging kids to participate and form their interest in tennis.

Vedant Chavan is a rising junior at Interlake High School and is a coach for the Seattle branch. He has always been passionate about tennis ever since he started playing at a young age. 
"Coaching the game gives me the opportunity to share my passion with kids. I believe that coaching is teaching players not only the skills required to play the game, but also hard work, determination, focus, and respect which are values applicable on the court and in their lives. I look forward to meeting and coaching more kids at our events!"

Sylvia Eklund is a senior at Interlake High School. She started playing tennis when she was 7 years old and competes regularly in USTA tournaments. She played varsity tennis at Interlake high school and won state for doubles in 2018 and 2019. 
"I’m excited to be coaching because I want others to have the opportunities I have had through tennis and be able to give back to the community."

Addie Eklund is a senior at Interlake High school. She has been playing tennis and competing in tournaments since she was 7. In addition to USTA, she played on the varsity tennis team at Interlake high school where they won state two years in a row and she placed third in singles individually both years. 
"I enjoy coaching because tennis is such a big part of my life and I enjoy sharing my passion with others."

Helen Le is a coach for the Texas branch who has been playing tennis for many years and loves to interact with others through the sport. She loves to spend time with kids and help cultivate their interest in tennis as well. She also has experience volunteering at sports and tennis camps run by her school.

Sohan Jayanth is a rising senior at Dougherty Valley High School in San Ramon, California. He is currently a varsity player on the Dougherty team roster, and was the former junior varsity captain. 
“I’m really looking forward to coaching because tennis has been a big passion of mine for such a long time and I’m excited to get to share that passion with the youth in my community and possibly inspire a new generation of tennis players.”
